Study Methods

1. Pomodoro Technique

How it Works: Work for 25 minutes, then take a 5- minute break. After four cycles, take a longer break (15-30 minutes).

Benefits: Helps maintain focus, prevents burnout, and makes long tasks more manageable.

Use our pomodoro timer to help!

2. Eisenhower Matrix

How it Works: Categorize tasks into four quadrants based on urgency and importance:

  • Urgent and Important: Do it immediately.
  • Important but Not Urgent: Schedule it.
  • Urgent but Not Important: Delegate it.
    • Not Urgent and Not Important: Eliminate it.

Benefits: Prioritizes tasks, helping you focus on what's truly important.

Use our to-do list to help!

3. 90-Minute Work Cycles

How it Works: Work in cycles of 90 minutes followed by a 20-30 minute break, aligning with the body's natural ultradian rhythm.

Benefits: Matches natural energy levels, reducing fatigue and improving focus.

4. Feynman Technique

How it Works: Teach a concept you are learning to someone else in simple terms. If you struggle, go back and review the material.

Benefits: Deepens understanding and highlights gaps in knowledge.
